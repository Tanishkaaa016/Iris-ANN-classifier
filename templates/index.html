00 — Dataset

About the Iris Dataset

Introduced by British statistician Ronald Fisher in 1936, the Iris dataset is one of the most famous benchmarks in machine learning. It contains measurements from 150 iris flowers across three species — each uniquely distinguishable by the shape and size of their petals and sepals. The challenge: can a model learn to tell them apart with just four numbers per flower?

Iris Setosa
Class 0 · Easily separable

The most distinctive of the three. Its small, compact petals make it trivially separable from the others — even a linear classifier can identify it perfectly. Found in Arctic and subarctic regions.
